# Competitive Landscape Mapper

## Purpose

Build a comprehensive map of the competitive landscape to understand who you are up against and where there may be whitespace.  Categorising competitors and analysing their strengths and weaknesses helps you position your offering and pre‑empt strategic threats.

## When to use

Use during the Idea Stage and whenever entering a new market or segment.  Maintain and update the map over time; the landscape evolves as incumbents pivot, new entrants emerge and adjacent players expand.

## Inputs

- Problem hypothesis
- Keywords describing the solution

## Process

1. **Generate a competitor list.**  Use the problem hypothesis and relevant keywords to search for:
   - **Direct competitors** solving the same problem for the same customer segment.
   - **Indirect competitors** solving the problem via alternative approaches or adjacent workflows.
   - **Adjacent players and potential acquirers** in neighbouring markets who could enter or absorb your space.
2. **Classify and describe.**  For each competitor, capture core offerings, customer segments, pricing models, distribution channels, funding stage and unique selling points.
3. **Analyse competitor advantage.**  Ask AI to argue why each competitor could succeed instead of you.  Consider brand equity, data moats, network effects, regulatory capture and access to distribution.
4. **Identify differentiators and gaps.**  Compare your emerging solution concept against the competitor matrix.  Highlight areas where competitors are strong or weak and where underserved needs remain.  Summarise potential moats or differentiators.
5. **Assess market dynamics.**  Extend the analysis by asking AI to estimate total addressable market (TAM), serviceable available market (SAM) and serviceable obtainable market (SOM).  Map buyer landscapes, industry consolidation trends, and headwinds/tailwinds (e.g. technological, demographic, regulatory or macroeconomic factors) relevant to the space.
6. **Plan follow‑up research.**  Identify unknowns or high‑impact uncertainties that require deeper research or customer interviews.

## Output

This skill returns a **competitive landscape report** containing:

- Categorised lists of direct, indirect and adjacent competitors.
- Descriptions of each competitor’s offerings, target segments and strategic advantages.
- Analysis of why competitors might win and where they are vulnerable.
- Summary of market size estimates and trend tailwinds/headwinds.
- Suggested differentiators and areas for further investigation.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Purpose is clear and specific
- [ ] Inputs are identified and complete
- [ ] Steps are actionable and unambiguous
- [ ] Expected output is well defined
- [ ] Failure modes are considered

## Failure modes

- Relying solely on superficial web searches; missing stealth competitors or new entrants.
- Overlooking non‑obvious substitutes that solve the same job-to-be-done.
- Ignoring macroeconomic or regulatory headwinds that could impact all players.
- Treating the landscape as static instead of updating as the market evolves.
