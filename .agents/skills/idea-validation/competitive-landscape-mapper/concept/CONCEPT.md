# Concept

    ## Original intention
    Build a comprehensive map of the competitive landscape to understand who you are up against and where there may be whitespace.  Categorising competitors and analysing their strengths and weaknesses helps you position your offering and pre‑empt strategic threats.

    ## Strategic value
    This concept helps teams produce consistent outcomes using Codex instead of ad-hoc prompts.

    ## Primary use cases
    Use during the Idea Stage and whenever entering a new market or segment.  Maintain and update the map over time; the landscape evolves as incumbents pivot, new entrants emerge and adjacent players expand.

    ## User problems solved
    - Reduces ambiguity when executing competitive-landscape-mapper.
    - Converts high-level intent into structured, reusable outputs.

    ## Expected transformation
    Input context becomes a validated, structured artifact aligned with the skill objective.

    ## Risks and limitations
    - Relying solely on superficial web searches; missing stealth competitors or new entrants.
- Overlooking non‑obvious substitutes that solve the same job-to-be-done.
- Ignoring macroeconomic or regulatory headwinds that could impact all players.
- Treating the landscape as static instead of updating as the market evolves.

    ## Future extensions
    - Add stronger domain-specific validation rules.
    - Expand generated templates for advanced scenarios.
